# Product

## Register

product

> Scope note. This document describes the platform-wide design language and educational direction for all Simatrix engineering simulations. This version is adapted for Engineering Mechanics Module 1 simulations.

---

# Users

Primary persona: the struggling first-year B.Tech student who finds Engineering Mechanics abstract, mathematical, and intimidating.

The simulations must:

* visually explain force systems
* connect equations with physical intuition
* reduce fear of mechanics
* guide students step-by-step

The learner may:

* have weak mathematics background
* struggle with vector visualization
* confuse equilibrium concepts
* fear engineering drawings and force systems

The simulation must teach without requiring prior CAD or simulation knowledge.

---

# Product Purpose

Simatrix Engineering Mechanics simulations help students build intuition for:

* vectors
* force systems
* moments
* couples
* equilibrium
* support reactions
* beam loading

using interactive guided visualizations.

The platform follows a Guided Stepper approach:

* one idea at a time
* progressive disclosure
* concept-first learning
* visual intuition before equations

Each simulation follows this educational arc:

1. Orient
2. Visualize
3. Experiment
4. Solve textbook-style problems

---

# Brand Personality

Three words:

* patient
* encouraging
* clear

The simulation behaves like:

* a calm tutor
* not a game
* not CAD software
* not a toy

The interface must:

* feel professional
* remain beginner-friendly
* avoid intimidation

---

# Educational Goals

Engineering Mechanics simulations should help students visually understand:

* force direction
* vector resolution
* resultant forces
* rotational effects
* equilibrium conditions
* beam reactions
* distributed loads

The simulation must connect mathematical formulas with physical understanding.

---

# Design Principles

1. Design for weak students first.
2. Reveal one idea at a time.
3. Keep UI consistent across all Simatrix subjects.
4. Always show real values and units.
5. Use proper engineering vocabulary.
6. Make simulations accessible.
7. Keep the viewport as the main focus.

---

# Simulation Categories

Module 1 simulations include:

1. Vector Resolution
2. Resultant Force System
3. Moment of Force
4. Couple System
5. Equilibrium of Forces
6. Beam Support Reactions

---

# Interaction Philosophy

Students should:

* drag vectors
* change force magnitude
* rotate force direction
* visualize components
* apply loads
* observe equilibrium dynamically

Every interaction must:

* immediately affect the visualization
* update equations live
* reinforce the concept visually

---

# Accessibility

Every color-coded meaning must also include:

* labels
* line styles
* arrow styles
* thickness changes
* dashed/solid distinction

All simulations must:

* support keyboard interaction
* support reduced motion
* maintain readable contrast
* provide visible focus states

---

# Emotional Goal

The learner should feel:

“I can actually understand mechanics.”

The simulation should reduce fear and increase confidence while preserving engineering seriousness.
